Imatinib — a small-molecule tyrosine kinase inhibitor, the starting point of the path.
“Imatinib binds the ATP-binding pocket of the BCR-ABL tyrosine kinase, locking it in an inactive conformation and blocking its catalytic activity.”
DrugBank — Mechanism of action
View source ↗
BCR-ABL — the constitutively active fusion kinase produced by the Philadelphia-chromosome translocation.
“The constitutively activated BCR-ABL tyrosine kinase drives the uncontrolled proliferation of myeloid progenitor cells that defines chronic myeloid leukemia.”
Efficacy and Safety of a Specific Inhibitor of the BCR-ABL Tyrosine Kinase in Chronic Myeloid Leukemia
Druker BJ, et al. · New England Journal of Medicine · 2001
PMID 11287972 ↗
Cell population proliferation — the unchecked growth signal BCR-ABL sustains once active.
“Sustained proliferation of the BCR-ABL–positive clone underlies the progressive expansion of the leukemic cell population.”
The molecular biology of chronic myeloid leukemia
Deininger MW, Goldman JM, Melo JV · Blood · 2000
PMID 15618470 ↗
Chronic myeloid leukemia — the malignant expansion imatinib halts by shutting down its molecular driver. Blocking BCR-ABL decreases disease progression.
